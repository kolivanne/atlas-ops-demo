# Confluence Governance & Strategy

## Goals
- Improve knowledge accessibility
  - Measure: search success rate, page views, time to find content
- Remove outdated content
  - Review pages not updated in 6 months
  - Archive pages not updated in 12 months
- Establish clear ownership
  - Assign space and critical page owners
  - Track ownership via Confluence metadata

## Structure
- **Space Ownership**
  - Each space has a primary owner responsible for content quality
  - Owners review content quarterly
- **Page Templates**
  - Standard templates for Policies, How-Tos, Meeting Notes
  - Templates updated annually to reflect best practices
- **Naming Conventions**
  - Use `[Project/Team] - [Topic] - [Document Type]` format
  - Enforce consistent naming via training and review

## Lifecycle Management
- **Stale Content Identification**
  - Pages not updated in 6 months → notify owner
  - Pages not updated in 12 months → archive to designated archive space
- **Actions**
  - Notifications automated using Confluence macros or scheduled emails
  - Archive process: move to archive space, restrict editing, mark as `Archived`
  - Owners can request exemptions if content is still relevant

## Audit & Review
- Quarterly content audits by space owners
- KPI tracking:
  - % of pages updated within last 12 months
  - Search success rate
  - User feedback score on content usefulness

## Communication & Training
- Announce governance rules to all teams via Confluence
- Provide training sessions on templates, naming conventions, and ownership
- Document governance playbook in a dedicated Confluence space
